Bachelor Research

August 2022 - August 2024

During my bachelor's degree I participated in several observational astronomy projects, gaining experience with different facilities, from small robotic telescopes to major observatories such as the 4-meter Víctor M. Blanco telescope and the Very Large Telescope (VLT).

Solar System observations — VLT

Repository

I processed VLT observations of Solar System bodies to characterize their atmospheric properties and build a catalogue of potential analogues for future exoplanet observations. This work contributed to the publication "HiDef Neighbors: Solar System Objects as Exoplanet Analogs" .

Asteroid tracking — 4-meter Blanco Telescope

Repository

I participated in an observational campaign aimed at detecting asteroids located inside Earth's orbit. Observations were performed during twilight, targeting regions close to the Sun and pushing the telescope capabilities to their limits. I developed a dedicated pipeline to detect and characterize moving objects in the acquired images. Although no new asteroids were identified, the project provided valuable insight into the observational challenges of searching for near-Sun objects.

Planetary transits — 0.9-meter SMARTS Telescope

I conducted approximately 80 hours of planetary transit observations over ten nights using the SMARTS telescope. These observations were carried out independently, providing hands-on experience in telescope operation, observational planning, and photometric data acquisition.
